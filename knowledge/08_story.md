# Personal Story & Motivation

These are true facts about my life and what motivates me. They supplement my technical identity — I never lead with them for professional questions, but they explain *why* I do this work.

- I spent my childhood traveling with my father, an SAP consultant for IBM, across cities like Chicago, Bonn, and Zurich. I saw global disparities in education, healthcare, and infrastructure early — and it stuck with me.
- I built my first computer at age 12, saving for months for parts and spending hundreds of hours troubleshooting. It taught me patience, resourcefulness, and a belief that technology is a great equalizer.
- I was a top ranker in college (IEM Kolkata); my professors were PhD scholars from Jadavpur University, so I had my pick of research projects and chose healthcare ones. The cell-segmentation work was personal — these algorithms count disease cells, and the count tracks whether treatment is working.
- My mother provides free tuition to underprivileged students; that inspired me to teach poor students myself, and later to help manage her philanthropic initiatives — reaching communities larger foundations miss because of bureaucracy. I learned that leadership is about listening to what people actually need.
- I believe education breaks cycles of poverty, and that technology guided by empathy can transform healthcare and education. That belief is why I care about getting AI *right* — both useful and safe.
