# Work Ethic, Capacity & How I Operate

## I carry a lot at once — right now
At this moment I'm running **three demanding responsibilities in parallel**, and doing each one well:
1. **Sole ML engineer at Varosync** — owning a production molecular-similarity pipeline end to end.
2. **Teaching Co-Lead of the AIDE program** — designing and delivering a 12-session ML curriculum for PhD students.
3. **My M.S. at Northeastern** — maintaining a 4.0 GPA — plus the **BlueDot Technical AI Safety** cohort on top.

These aren't light commitments stacked for show; each has real deliverables and real people depending on it. I'm comfortable operating at that load, and I prioritize ruthlessly to keep quality high across all of them.

## I've done this historically, not just now
- Ranked **2nd out of 180+** in my B.Tech while simultaneously doing healthcare research at CMATER Lab and a remote CV research internship.
- **7 years** of consistent volunteer teaching at The Hope Foundation alongside a full course load — sustained effort over years, not a one-off.
- Mentored junior researchers and accelerated their timelines while doing my own research.

## What I want from a role: the hardest problems available
I'm not looking for comfortable work. I want a team to **hand me their toughest problems** — the ambiguous ones with no clear playbook, the ones that need someone to define the metric, build the pipeline, and push until it works. That's exactly the environment I thrive in: being the sole ML engineer at an early-stage startup, or proving a subtle causal claim in interpretability. I learn fastest and do my best work when the problem is genuinely hard.

## How I work
- **Ownership end-to-end:** I'll take a problem from data and labels through training, evaluation, and serving — and own the outcome, not just my slice.
- **Rigor:** I check causally, test counterfactually, and report honestly — including negative results. I'd rather surface a real gap than paper over it.
- **Speed without sloppiness:** I use agentic coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor) to move fast as a one-person team, but I keep the quality bar high.
- **I ramp myself:** drop me into an unfamiliar stack and I'll get productive quickly — that's been the pattern across research labs, a startup, and self-driven safety study.
