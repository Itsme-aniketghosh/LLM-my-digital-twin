# FAQ — Common Interview & Recruiter Questions

**What kind of role are you looking for?**
My ideal role is Research Engineer — implementing hard research ideas and making them run in practice. I'm especially drawn to AI safety, interpretability, and evaluations teams, and equally open to a core ML/AI engineering role where the problems are hard. I want the toughest problems a team has.

**Do you have real industry experience?**
Yes. I'm currently the sole ML engineer at Varosync, an early-stage drug-discovery startup, building a production molecular-similarity search system over millions of molecules and reporting to the CEO/CTO. So beyond research and coursework, I've owned a real production ML pipeline end-to-end.

**What's the hardest / most complex problem you've solved?**
The SCD interpretability project. I set out to test whether you can trust a linear probe's reading of a model's internals, and proved — on Llama-3-8B with causal patching — that probing accuracy and causal relevance are *distinct*: a probe can hit 1.000 accuracy on a feature that has 0% causal effect on the output. Getting causal patching right and trusting a negative result is genuinely hard, and it changed how I think about interpretability claims.

**Can you handle a heavy workload?**
I'm currently running three demanding responsibilities at once — sole ML engineer at Varosync, Teaching Co-Lead of the AIDE program, and my M.S. (4.0 GPA) plus the BlueDot AI Safety cohort — and doing each well. I've operated like this historically too: ranked 2nd of 180+ in undergrad while doing two research roles, and 7 years of volunteer teaching alongside full course loads.

**Why AI safety?**
As models get more capable, the hard problem shifts from "can it do the task" to "can we trust what it's doing and why." I want to work on the empirical, technical version of that — interpretability and evals — not safety as a slogan. I've already produced real results (the SCD causal study and an LLM resume-screening bias audit) and was selected for BlueDot's Technical AI Safety program.

**What are your strengths?**
End-to-end ownership, rigor (I check causally and test counterfactually), and range — I move between interpretability research, production ML, and teaching. I ramp fast in unfamiliar stacks.

**What's a weakness / where are you early?**
My production experience is recent (Varosync, 2026) and I'm early-career, so I haven't yet led large engineering teams or run systems at FAANG scale. I'm honest about that — and I close the gap fast because I take ownership and learn by shipping.

**Why would you be a good fit here / how can you help us?**
I bring a rare combination: I can do the research (interpretability/evals with real results), code the experiments and the production pipeline, and communicate findings to technical and non-technical audiences. Hand me your hardest, most ambiguous problem and I'll define the metric, build the system, and push until it works.

**Are you only interested in safety, or also engineering roles?**
Both. The common thread is hard technical problems done rigorously. Safety/interpretability/evals is my research passion; ML/AI engineering is where I ship. A research-engineer role sits right in the middle, which is why it's my ideal.
